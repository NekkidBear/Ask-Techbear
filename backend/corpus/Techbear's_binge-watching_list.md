# TechBear's Binge-Watching List

A collection of recurring television shows, films, and entertainment properties frequently referenced throughout Ask TechBear and Guide to the Multiverse stories.

---

## Workplace Comedies

### "Priority 0!"

**Inspired by:** Emergency! (1970s)

**Premise:** A rapid-response team of mobile field technicians races from crisis to crisis responding to catastrophic IT failures.

**Recurring themes:** Users who swear they pressed the button. Executive escalations. Printer emergencies. After-hours service calls. "It was working yesterday."

**Notable characters:** Connie (Dispatcher), TechBear (Field Technician)

**Running joke:** The problem is almost never what was reported.

---

### Project Server Room

**Inspired by:** Project Runway

**Premise:** Contestants are given a collection of obsolete hardware, mystery cables, incomplete documentation, and impossible business requirements. They must create a functioning enterprise network before the deadline.

**Typical challenge:** "You have: one VAX, one Windows ME tower, an aging PBX, two unknown cables. Management wants cloud services and VOIP by Friday. Make it work."

**Running joke:** The judges are impressed by solutions that should not be physically possible.

---

### W.R. (Workplace Request)

**Inspired by:** ER

**Premise:** A dramatic series following cybersecurity analysts, PFYs, engineers, and technicians defending a Fortune 500 honeypot from constant cyber attacks.

**Recurring themes:** Ransomware. Incident response. Digital kudzu outbreaks. Exhausted engineers. Midnight crisis calls.

**Running joke:** Every incident becomes a Priority One emergency.

---

## Sitcoms

### L\*I\*N\*U\*X

**Inspired by:** Friends

**Premise:** A group of infrastructure specialists navigate careers, relationships, and increasingly questionable production deployments.

**Famous episode:** "We Were In A Code Freeze!"

**Running joke:** Ross repeatedly deploys directly to production before long weekends.

---

### How I Met Your Motherboard?

**Inspired by:** How I Met Your Mother

**Premise:** A veteran systems engineer tells increasingly implausible stories about how he met various pieces of enterprise hardware.

**Running joke:** Every story takes far longer than necessary.

---

### The IT Crowd (Multiversal Edition)

**Inspired by:** The IT Crowd

**Premise:** The familiar support department, but operating across multiple dimensions.

**Running joke:** No matter the universe, users still ignore error messages.

---

## Dramas

### Law & Order: Technical Services Division

**Inspired by:** Law & Order

**Premise:** Investigators solve crimes involving cybercrime, data breaches, fraud, and suspicious spreadsheet activity.

**Opening narration:** "In the technology system, digital offenses are considered especially heinous..."

**Running joke:** The culprit is usually poor password management.

---

### CyberScams She Wrote

**Inspired by:** Murder, She Wrote

**Premise:** An elderly cybersecurity consultant repeatedly stumbles into online scams, fraud rings, and phishing schemes.

**Running joke:** She identifies the scam before the authorities do.

---

### The Silicon Valley Girls

**Inspired by:** The Golden Girls

**Premise:** A group of retired programmers, engineers, and startup veterans share a house while offering unsolicited technology advice.

**Running joke:** Every conversation eventually becomes an argument about programming languages.

---

## Reality Television

### Real Housewives of Silicon Valley

**Inspired by:** Real Housewives

**Premise:** Tech executives, venture capitalists, and startup founders compete for status, funding, and conference speaking slots.

**Running joke:** The drama is more unstable than their production environments.

---

### Real Bitcoin Miners of Silicon Valley

**Inspired by:** Reality competition series

**Premise:** Cryptocurrency enthusiasts compete to maximize mining output while arguing about cooling systems and electrical bills.

**Running joke:** Nobody is actually making money.

---

### RuPaul's Tech Race

**Inspired by:** RuPaul's Drag Race

**Premise:** Contestants compete in challenges requiring technical skill, presentation ability, improvisation, and creative problem-solving.

**GRUNT criteria:** Glamour, Resourcefulness, Unflappability, Natural Language Processing, Talent

**Running joke:** The challenge is never what the contestants prepared for.

---

## Science & Technology Programming

### Tech News Tonight

**Inspired by:** Technology news programs

**Premise:** A nightly roundup of technology headlines, product launches, security incidents, and industry gossip.

**Running joke:** Every "revolutionary innovation" is just a slightly different subscription service.

---

### Data Center Island

**Inspired by:** Gilligan's Island

**Premise:** A group of IT professionals become stranded in an isolated data center after a catastrophic infrastructure failure.

**Running joke:** They can build impossible technical solutions but cannot leave.

---

## Historical & Documentary Programming

### The Golden Devs

**Inspired by:** The Golden Girls

**Premise:** Veteran programmers gather to reminisce about punch cards, COBOL, floppy disks, and debugging techniques from previous decades.

**Running joke:** The "old way" somehow still works better.

---

## Unproduced / In-Universe Concepts

### The Nigerian Prince of Bel-Air

**Inspired by:** The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air

**Premise:** A suspiciously wealthy prince arrives with an unbelievable investment opportunity.

**Running joke:** Nobody should trust him, yet somebody always does.

---

## Shared Universe Canon

All TechBear television programs share the following assumptions:

- Documentation is missing.
- Someone ignored change control.
- Coffee is mission critical.
- Every workplace contains at least one Kevin.
- Technical debt eventually becomes the plot.
- The users remain unchanged regardless of century, planet, or dimension.
